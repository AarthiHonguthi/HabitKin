# HabitKin — How It Works
### The complete journey from download to Day 30

---

## The two roles — in plain English

Every person on HabitKin plays one of two roles on each habit:

**Keeper** — the person *doing* the habit. You log it every day. You own it.

**Kin** — the person *watching*. You see if the Keeper logged today. You can send one tap to say "I'm watching." You don't judge, you just witness.

> The name HabitKin comes from this. Your Kin are the people who keep you honest.

**The key thing:** these roles are per habit, not per person.

You can be a Keeper of your own water habit while being a Kin for your friend's exercise habit — at the same time, in the same app. You switch views, you don't switch accounts.

---

## Day 1 — You download the app

You open HabitKin for the first time.

**Step 1 — Phone number login.** No email, no password. OTP to your number. Done in 20 seconds.

**Step 2 — Create your first habit.**
The app asks: what do you want to build?
You type: "Drink 2 glasses of water in the morning."
You pick how often: Daily.
You pick a reminder time: 8 AM.

**Step 3 — The most important question:**
> *"Is there someone in your life who you want to keep you honest on this?"*

Two options:
- Yes — invite them
- No — I'll start solo

If you tap Yes, the app generates a WhatsApp message you send to that person:
> *"Hey, I'm trying to build a water habit. I added you as my Kin on HabitKin — you'll see if I actually do it every day. [link]"*

They tap the link, sign up in 30 seconds, and they're now your Kin on this habit. **This is how the app grows — every Keeper brings in at least one Kin.**

If they don't have the app yet, no problem. They get a WhatsApp message summary every Sunday anyway (if you set this up). They don't need to be active in the app every day. More on this later.

---

## Day 1 to Day 7 — The basic loop

Every day looks like this for you (the Keeper):

1. At 8 AM — your reminder arrives. One tap in the notification to log. Done. You don't even need to open the app.
2. Your Kin sees a small update: *"Aarthi logged Water ✓ today"*
3. If your Kin thinks you need a push, they tap one button: **Nudge**. You get a notification: *"Your mom is watching 👀"*
4. That nudge makes you 2x more likely to log. We know this. We're already measuring it from Day 1.

Every day looks like this for your Kin:

1. They open the app (or don't — they get a WhatsApp summary on Sunday either way)
2. They see your streak, your check-ins, whether you logged today
3. One button: Nudge. That's it. No commenting, no judging, no posting.

**From Day 1, the app is silently recording:**
- Did you log today? Yes / No
- Did your Kin nudge you today? Yes / No
- How many minutes after the nudge did you log?
- What day of the week do you tend to skip?
- Was your Kin active this week?

You don't see this data yet. It's building up in the background. This is the foundation of everything that comes on Day 21.

---

## Day 7 — The streak milestone

After 7 consecutive days, the app shows you a small moment:

> *"7 days straight. Your Kin saw every single one."*

No points. No badges. Just a real statement. Your streak is now visible to your Kin too — they feel part of it.

---

## Day 7 to Day 14 — Both of you can now flip roles

Here's where it gets interesting.

After the first week, the app asks your Kin:
> *"Want to build a habit yourself? Aarthi can be your Kin."*

Your Kin can now create their own habit and add you as their Kin. Now you're watching them while they watch you.

**This is the role flip.** On Habit A (Water), you are the Keeper and your mom is the Kin. On Habit B (her evening walk), she is the Keeper and you are the Kin. Same two people. Two habits. Both accountable to each other.

The app home screen has two sections:

```
MY HABITS           I'M WATCHING
────────────        ────────────
Water ✓ 14d         Mom's Walk ✗ today
Exercise ✓ 3d       Friend's Reading ✓
```

You can have habits where you're the Keeper and habits where you're the Kin — all in one place.

---

## Day 14 — The first honest signal

By now the app has 14 days of data on you. Enough to run a basic calculation — not a fancy correlation, just honest arithmetic:

```
Days your Kin nudged you:     8 days → you logged 7 of them (87%)
Days no nudge:                6 days → you logged 3 of them (50%)
```

The app knows this. You don't see it yet. But this number is real and it belongs to you.

This is called the **nudge effect** — and it is the first thing that gets revealed on Day 21.
